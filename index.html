// Pro tips for experienced engineers
01
Think out loud — always
Interviewers grade your communication as much as your code. Narrate your thought process, edge cases, and tradeoffs even when you're completely stuck. Silence is the worst answer.
02
Patterns over problems
Don't memorise 500 solutions. Learn 15–20 core patterns (sliding window, BFS/DFS, two pointers, DP state machine) and you can solve most problems you've never seen before.
03
Your 4+ years is an edge — use it
You have real production war stories. In system design and behavioural rounds, your experience scaling actual systems beats theoretical knowledge every time. Speak from what you've built.
04
Time-box every problem
Max 25 minutes on any LeetCode problem. If stuck, read hints, then the solution. Understanding patterns matters more than reaching solutions independently — that's not the real interview skill.
05
System design framework — always
Requirements → Estimates → API design → Data model → High-level design → Deep dive → Tradeoffs. Never skip straight to drawing boxes. Clarifying requirements alone sets you apart from 60% of candidates.
06
Apply early, not at Week 12
Start applying at Week 8–9. Interview timelines take 3–6 weeks from application to offer. You want your best offers arriving after your peak preparation, not before it.
07
Debrief every mock within 1 hour
The pattern of your mistakes is more valuable than any problem set. Track what went wrong in a doc. After 10 mocks, you'll see your actual weak areas — not the ones you assumed.
08
Never accept the first offer
With 4+ years of experience, you have strong leverage. Counter every offer with levels.fyi data. At senior levels in India, ₹5–20L/year can often be reclaimed in a single 10-minute call.
